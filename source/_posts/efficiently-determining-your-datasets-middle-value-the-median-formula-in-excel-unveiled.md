##  What Is the Median in Excel?

 The median is the number in the middle of a sorted set of numbers. For example, if your numbers are 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5, your median is 3\. Or, if your numbers are 1, 8, and 9, the median is 8.

 However, in the case that the count of numbers in your series is even, the average of the two middle numbers is considered the median. Notably, the median is different from [the mean](https://screen-video-capture.techidaily.com/updated-in-2024-mastering-your-first-zoom-webinar-with-ease/), which is [the average](https://win-able.techidaily.com/fixing-overwatch-startup-issues-how-to-get-rid-of-the-persistent-black-screen/) of a set of numbers.

 Keep in mind that Excel ignores any [empty cells](https://desktop-recording.techidaily.com/2024-approved-vdg-screen-snatcher-synopsis-full-breakdown/) while calculating the median.
